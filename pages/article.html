A PAPAYA 1-2

SUCCESS AT SILVERSTONE

MCLARENS' SILVERSTONE MASTERCLASS: NORRIS & PIASTRI SHINE IN 1–2

The 2025 British Grand Prix delivered exactly what British fans had dreamed of: a historic 1–2 finish for McLaren. Lando Norris powered to an emotional home victory, while teammate Oscar Piastri secured second—albeit with controversy surrounding a late-race penalty.

RACE RECAP

• It's a clean start for Oscar and Lando as they maintain their positions

• Oscar takes the lead from Verstappen as the track dries

• Rain returns, and the team executes a double stack for fresh Intermediate tyres

• A slower stop for Lando means Max overtakes him in the pit lane

• The Safety Car is deployed for heavy rain and the race eventually resumes

• Another Safety Car is deployed after an incident; Verstappen spins, and P2 is inherited by Lando

• A 10-second penalty is given to Oscar for a Safety Car infringement

• Oscar boxes to serve the penalty and switch to Medium tyres, rejoining in P2

• Lando boxes for Medium tyres and maintains the lead

• The chequered flag flies, and it's a McLaren 1-2 at the British Grand Prix!

LANDO

“It was hard to imagine what this would be like – but it’s everything I dreamed of, everything I've ever wanted to achieve. Aside from winning a Championship, I think this is as good as it gets in terms of feelings, in terms of achievement. It was an incredible race, but the support of the fans made the difference today. The last few laps, I was looking into the crowd, trying to take it all in and enjoy the moment. Shout out to my grandstand in the corner. They were amazing. They cheered me on every lap, and it was really a beautiful thing. “As for the race itself, it was about as stressful as they can get. It’s a long 52 laps, you never knew what was going to happen, especially when it was raining earlier. But the team made good decisions, we pitted at the right times and got an amazing result. Thank you to the team for their incredible work - these are memories I’ll have forever.”

OSCAR

“Today didn’t end how I wanted it to for me but well done to the team on a really successful day. A 1-2 at our home Grand Prix is brilliant and it’s a great chunk of points for us. Whilst it was a frustrating one for me with the penalty, we had a rocket ship of a car, so thank you to the team for all their work which meant we could pull such a gap over the rest of the field. I’ll use the frustration of today to fuel us further at the next weekend, but for now, we can reflect on a great weekend for McLaren.”
