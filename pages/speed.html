DECADES OF SPEED

LEGENDS FROM DIFFERENT TIMES

EMERSON FITTIPALDI (1974–1975)

Already a World Champion with Lotus, Fittipaldi joined McLaren and delivered the team’s first-ever Drivers' Title in 1974. Known for his smooth style and calculated driving, he helped elevate McLaren into a championship contender.

JAMES HUNT (1976-1978)

The ultimate racing rebel. Hunt won the 1976 title in dramatic fashion, overcoming rival Niki Lauda in one of F1’s most intense seasons. Fast, fiery, and unapologetically bold, James Hunt gave McLaren one of its most iconic moments.

ALAIN PROST (1984-1989)

Nicknamed The Professor for his intelligent and strategic racing style. Prost won three titles with McLaren and was known for consistency, precision, and mastering every detail. His fierce rivalry with Senna defined a golden age of F1.

AYRTON SENNA (1988-1993)

A force of nature. Senna won three World Championships with McLaren and became a global icon. Known for his blinding speed, daring pole laps, and emotional connection with fans, Senna remains one of the greatest drivers in F1 history.

MIKA HAKKINEN (1993-2001)

Cool under pressure and lightning-fast. Häkkinen gave McLaren its next golden era, winning back-to-back titles in 1998 and 1999. His rivalry with Michael Schumacher is legendary, and his calm persona made him a fan favorite.

LEWIS HAMILTON (2007-2012)

Burst onto the F1 scene with McLaren in 2007, nearly winning the title as a rookie. In 2008, he became the youngest World Champion at the time, securing McLaren’s last title to date. Known for raw talent, racecraft, and breaking barriers.
